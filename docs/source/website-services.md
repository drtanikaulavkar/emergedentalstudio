## **Dental Implants:**

Dental implants are artificial tooth roots that are used to replace missing teeth. They are surgically implanted into the jawbone and provide a stable base for a crown or bridge to be placed on top. Dental implants are a popular option for people who have lost one or more teeth due to injury, decay, or other dental issues.

Benefits of Dental Implants:

1. Improved Appearance: Dental implants look and feel like natural teeth, improving the appearance of your smile and facial structure.
2. Durability: With proper care and maintenance, dental implants can last a lifetime, making them a cost-effective solution for missing teeth.
3. Improved Functionality: Dental implants function just like natural teeth, allowing you to eat and speak with confidence and without any discomfort.
4. Improved Oral Health: Dental implants help to maintain the health of your remaining teeth by preventing them from shifting and causing bite issues.
5. Convenience: Unlike dentures, which need to be removed and cleaned regularly, dental implants are permanent and require no special care beyond regular brushing and flossing.

The Dental Implant Process:

The dental implant process involves several steps:

1. Consultation: Evaluating your oral health and determining if dental implants are the right option for you. This Is also followed by regular and 3D X-rays for better planning and prognosis of the treatment.
2. Preparation: If necessary, preparing your mouth for the implant placement by removing any damaged teeth or gum tissue. Also, if necessary, surgeries for bone grafting or sinus lift procedures depending on the bone condition.
3. Implant Placement: Surgical placement of the dental implant into your jawbone.
4. Healing: It will take several weeks (approximately 3 months) for the implant to fuse with your jawbone and heal properly.
5. Abutment Placement: Once the implant has healed, visit to attach an abutment to the implant to hold the crown or bridge in place. This includes a second stage surgery followed by mouth impressions.
6. Crown or Bridge Placement: Finally, attachment of a crown or bridge to the abutment, completing the dental implant process.

Aftercare:

It is important to take good care of your dental implants to ensure their longevity. This includes regular brushing and flossing, as well as regular dental check-ups and cleanings.

If you are interested in dental implants, consult with us to determine if they are the right option for you.

## **Root canal treatments:**

A root canal treatment, also known as endodontic therapy, is a dental procedure that is performed to save a damaged or infected tooth. A root canal treatment is a safe and effective way to save a damaged or infected tooth and prevent the need for extraction.

When is a Root Canal Treatment Needed?

A root canal treatment may be needed when the pulp inside a tooth becomes inflamed or infected due to deep decay, repeated dental procedures, or trauma to the tooth. Common symptoms of an infected tooth include:

* Severe toothache
* Swelling in the gums or face
* Sensitivity to hot and cold temperatures
* Discoloration of the tooth
* Tenderness when chewing or biting

Benefits of a Root Canal Treatment:

1. Saves the Natural Tooth: A root canal treatment can save a damaged or infected tooth and prevent the need for extraction.
2. Relieves Pain: A root canal treatment can relieve severe tooth pain and discomfort caused by an infected tooth.
3. Prevents Further Damage: A root canal treatment can prevent further damage to the tooth and surrounding tissues by removing the infected pulp and sealing the space.
4. Restores Function: After a root canal treatment, the tooth can be restored with a crown or filling to restore its function and appearance.

The Root Canal Procedure:

1. Numbing the Area: Local anesthesia is administered to numb the area around the tooth.
2. Removing the Pulp: An opening is made in the top of the tooth to access the pulp, which is removed using specialized tools.
3. Cleaning the Root Canals: The root canals are cleaned and shaped to prepare them for filling.
4. Filling the Space: The root canals are filled with a biocompatible material to prevent further infection and decay.
5. Sealing the Tooth: The opening in the tooth is sealed with a filling or crown to restore its function and appearance.

After a root canal treatment, the tooth may be sore for a few days, but this can be managed with over-the-counter pain relievers. Good oral hygiene practices, such as brushing twice a day, flossing daily, and regular dental check-ups, can help prevent further dental issues and maintain the health of the treated tooth.

If you're experiencing severe tooth pain or other symptoms of an infected tooth, consult with us to determine if a root canal treatment is right for you.

## **Dentures:**

Dentures are removable prosthetic devices that are used to replace missing teeth and surrounding tissues. They are custom-made to fit your mouth and can be made from a variety of materials, such as acrylic resin, porcelain, or metal. Dentures are a safe and effective way to replace missing teeth and improve oral health.

Types of Dentures:

1. Complete Dentures: Complete dentures are used when all of the teeth in the upper or lower jaw are missing. They are designed to fit snugly over the gums.
2. Partial Dentures: Partial dentures are used when some natural teeth remain in the upper or lower jaw. They are designed to fit around the remaining teeth and can be attached to them using metal or flexible clasps.
3. Implant-Supported Dentures: Implant-supported dentures are attached to dental implants that are surgically placed in the jawbone. They provide a more secure fit and can improve chewing function.

Benefits of Dentures:

1. Improved Appearance: Dentures can improve the appearance of the smile and fill in gaps left by missing teeth.
2. Improved Chewing Function: Dentures can improve the ability to chew and eat a wider variety of foods.
3. Improved Speech: Dentures can improve speech by replacing missing teeth and supporting the structures of the mouth and face.
4. Improved Self-Confidence: Dentures can improve self-confidence by restoring a natural-looking smile.

Caring for Dentures:

1. Clean Them Daily: Dentures should be cleaned daily with a soft-bristled brush and denture cleaner to remove food particles and plaque.
2. Soak Them Overnight: Dentures should be soaked in water or denture cleaner overnight to prevent them from drying out and warping.
3. Handle with Care: Dentures are fragile and should be handled with care to prevent them from breaking.
4. Regular Dental Check-Ups: Regular dental check-ups are important to ensure that the dentures fit properly and to check for any potential issues.

If you're considering dentures, consult with us to determine the best course of action for you.

This treatment may involve a combination of procedures, such as splint therapy, restorative dentistry, cosmetic dentistry, and orthodontics, to address a wide range of dental issues.

When is a Full Mouth Rehabilitation Needed?

A full mouth rehabilitation may be needed when the teeth, gums, and jaw muscles and jaw joints are affected by a variety of dental issues, such as:

* Severe tooth decay
* Severe wearing off of teeth
* Generalised sensitivity
* Teeth clenching or grinding (bruxism)
* Gum disease
* Tooth loss
* Bite problems
* Misaligned teeth
* TMJ disorders and pain
* Facial trauma

Benefits of Full Mouth Rehabilitation:

1. Improved Oral Health: Full mouth rehabilitation can restore the health and function of the teeth, gums, and jaw, reducing the risk of further dental problems.
2. Improved Appearance: Full mouth rehabilitation can improve the appearance of the smile and facial features, enhancing self-confidence and self-esteem.
3. Improved Function: Full mouth rehabilitation can improve the ability to eat and speak comfortably, improving overall quality of life.
4. Personalized Treatment Plan: Full mouth rehabilitation is customized to meet the unique needs and goals of each individual patient, ensuring the best possible outcomes.

The Full Mouth Rehabilitation Process:

1. Comprehensive Evaluation: The first step in a full mouth rehabilitation is a comprehensive evaluation, which may include dental x-rays, impressions, and a thorough examination of the teeth, gums, and jaw muscles and jaw joints.
2. Treatment Planning: A customized treatment plan is developed based on the individual's needs and goals. This may involve a combination of procedures, such as dental implants, crowns, bridges, restorative fillings, orthodontics, or periodontal treatments.
3. Treatment: The full mouth rehabilitation process may take several months to complete, depending on the complexity of the case. The individual may need to undergo a series of procedures, such as tooth extractions, dental implants, and orthodontic treatments.
4. Follow-Up Care: After the full mouth rehabilitation is complete, the individual will need to maintain good oral hygiene practices and attend regular dental check-ups to ensure that the results are long-lasting.

## **Extractions & Impactions (Wisdom tooth removal):**

Tooth extraction is the process of removing a tooth from the mouth. Extractions may be necessary for a variety of reasons, such as severe tooth decay, gum disease, or trauma. Impacted teeth, which are teeth that are unable to fully emerge from the gums into the oral cavity, may also require extraction.

Types of Extractions:

1. Simple Extraction: Simple extraction is a routine procedure that is used to remove a tooth that is visible above the gumline. The area is numbed with a local anesthetic before using a specialized tool to loosen and remove the tooth.
2. Surgical Extraction: Surgical extraction is a more complex procedure that is used to remove a tooth that is impacted or hidden beneath the gumline. A small incision in the gum tissue is made to access the tooth, and the surrounding bone may need to be removed to extract the tooth.

Impacted Teeth:

Impacted teeth are teeth that are unable to fully emerge from the gums, either because they are blocked by other teeth or because they are growing in at an angle. Impacted teeth can cause pain, swelling, and infection, and may require extraction to prevent further complications.

Common Types of Impacted Teeth:

1. Wisdom Teeth: Wisdom teeth, or third molars, are the most commonly impacted teeth. They may need to be extracted if they are causing pain, infection, or other dental problems.
2. Canines: Canines, or cuspids, are the pointed teeth located between the incisors and premolars. Impacted canines can cause crowding and misalignment of the other teeth, and may require extraction or orthodontic treatment to correct.
3. Premolars: Premolars, or bicuspids, are the teeth located between the canines and molars. Impacted premolars can cause crowding and may require extraction or orthodontic treatment to correct.

Benefits of Extraction:

1. Relief from Pain and Discomfort: Extractions can provide relief from pain and discomfort caused by dental issues, such as tooth decay or gum disease.
2. Prevention of Further Complications: Extractions can prevent further complications, such as infection or damage to surrounding teeth.
3. Improved Oral Health: Extractions can improve oral health by removing damaged or diseased teeth and preventing the spread of infection.
4. Improved Alignment: Extraction of impacted teeth, such as wisdom teeth or canines, can improve alignment and prevent crowding of the other teeth.

Caring for the Extraction Site:

1. Bite Down Gently: Bite down gently on a gauze pad for 30-45 minutes after the extraction to stop bleeding.
2. Apply Ice: Apply ice to the affected area to reduce swelling and pain.
3. Avoid Solid, Hot and Spicy Foods: Avoid hard, hot and spicy food for the first 24 hours after the extraction, soft and semi solid diet is recommended for the first 2-3 days after extraction.
4. Avoid Smoking: for at least 24 hours after the extraction to prevent dry socket
5. Avoid Drinking any beverages with a straw
6. Follow other Instructions: Follow any specific instructions given specifically to you, for caring for the extraction site and managing any discomfort.

If you're experiencing dental pain or discomfort, consult with us to determine if an extraction or impaction is needed.

## **Teeth Cleaning & Teeth Whitening:**

Teeth cleaning: also known as prophylaxis, is a routine dental procedure that involves the removal of plaque, tartar, and stains from the teeth

Procedure:

1. Scaling: Using a specialized tool to remove plaque and tartar from the teeth and gumline.
2. Polishing: Using a polishing tool to smoothen the surface of teeth to prevent future tartar build up.
3. Flossing: Done between the teeth to remove any remaining debris.

Benefits of Teeth Cleaning:

1. Improved Oral Health: Teeth cleaning can improve oral health by removing plaque and tartar, which can lead to gum disease, cavities, and other dental problems.
2. Fresher Breath: Teeth cleaning can improve breath by removing bacteria and debris that can cause bad breath.
3. Early Detection of Dental Problems: During a teeth cleaning, detection can be done of early signs of dental problems, such as cavities, gum disease, and oral cancer.

Teeth whitening: is a cosmetic dental procedure that involves removing stains and discoloration from the teeth, resulting in a brighter, more vibrant smile.

Types of Teeth Whitening:

1. In-Office Whitening: In-office whitening is a procedure that is performed by a dentist in a dental office. The dentist applies a whitening solution to the teeth and uses a special light to activate the solution and accelerate the whitening process.
2. At-Home Whitening: At-home whitening involves the use of a custom-made tray that is filled with a whitening solution and worn for a specified amount of time each day.
3. Over-the-Counter Whitening: Over-the-counter whitening products, such as whitening toothpaste, whitening strips, and whitening gels, are available for purchase at drugstores and supermarkets.

Benefits of Teeth Whitening:

1. Improved Appearance: Teeth whitening can improve the appearance of your smile by removing stains and discoloration caused by aging, food and drink, smoking, and other factors.
2. Boosted Confidence: A brighter, more vibrant smile can boost your confidence and self-esteem, making you feel more comfortable and confident in social and professional situations.
3. Non-Invasive Procedure: Teeth whitening is a non-invasive procedure that can be completed in just one or two visits to the dentist.

Caring for Teeth After Cleaning & Whitening:

1. Brush and Floss Regularly: Brush and floss your teeth regularly to remove plaque and prevent the building up of new stains.
2. Avoid Staining Foods and Drinks: To maintain the results of teeth whitening, it's important to avoid consuming foods and drinks that can cause staining, such as coffee, tea, red wine, and dark-coloured fruits and vegetables.
3. Schedule Regular Dental Cleanings: Schedule regular dental cleanings with your dentist to remove any surface stains and maintain the health and appearance of your teeth.

Consult with us to determine how often you should schedule teeth cleanings based on your individual needs and dental history.
